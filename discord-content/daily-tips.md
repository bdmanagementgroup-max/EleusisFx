# #daily-tips — 30 Days of Trading Tips
# ══════════════════════════════════════════
# Post one per day. Short, punchy, valuable.

---

**Day 1** 💡
Risk management isn't optional — it's the entire game. The best prop firm traders risk 0.5-1.5% per trade. Never more. That's how you survive the losing streaks and still pass.

---

**Day 2** 💡
The London session (7am-12pm GMT) produces the most reliable moves on EUR/USD, GBP/USD, and EUR/GBP. If you only trade one session, make it this one.

---

**Day 3** 💡
Your stop loss should be placed at a level where your trade idea is WRONG — not at a level where you lose a comfortable amount. Structure first, position size second.

---

**Day 4** 💡
The first 15 minutes of any session is noise. Let the market show its hand before you enter. Patience isn't boring — it's profitable.

---

**Day 5** 💡
Never move your stop loss further away from entry. If you need a wider stop, your entry was wrong. Accept it and wait for the next setup.

---

**Day 6** 💡
3 trades per day. Maximum. If you can't find 3 quality setups, take fewer. If you've taken 3 and want more, close the charts. Overtrading is the #1 evaluation killer.

---

**Day 7** 💡
FTMO's daily drawdown resets at midnight CET — and it calculates from the HIGHER of your starting balance or highest equity that day. A big morning win can actually shrink your afternoon risk allowance.

---

**Day 8** 💡
The best trade is the one you don't take. Walking away from a mediocre setup is a skill. Protecting capital IS making money.

---

**Day 9** 💡
NFP (Non-Farm Payrolls) Friday = no trading. The spike is unpredictable, and prop firm evaluations aren't the place for gambling. Sit it out.

---

**Day 10** 💡
If you're down 2% on the day, stop trading. Period. No "one more trade to get it back." Professional evaluation traders have a hard daily loss limit and they never break it.

---

**Day 11** 💡
Your R:R ratio should be minimum 2:1. If your stop is 20 pips, your take profit should be at least 40 pips. Anything less and you need a 60%+ win rate just to break even.

---

**Day 12** 💡
Position size calculator: https://eleusisfx.uk/resources/position-size-calculator
Use it EVERY trade. Guessing lot sizes is how evaluations blow up.

---

**Day 13** 💡
The NY session overlap (12pm-4pm GMT) is the second best window. London + NY together create the highest liquidity = cleanest moves.

---

**Day 14** 💡
Don't trade the same pair in the same direction twice after a loss. If GBP/USD long hit your stop, don't immediately re-enter GBP/USD long. The market is telling you something.

---

**Day 15** 💡
Phase 2 of FTMO only needs 5% in 60 days. That's 0.08% per day. ONE decent trade per week gets you there. Don't overthink it.

---

**Day 16** 💡
Correlation kills accounts. GBP/USD and EUR/USD often move together. Going long both = double exposure. If one loses, both likely lose. Manage your portfolio, not just individual trades.

---

**Day 17** 💡
Your journal is more important than your indicator. Write down: entry reason, emotional state, session time, outcome. Review weekly. The patterns will shock you.

---

**Day 18** 💡
The Asian session (11pm-7am GMT) is generally low-volatility for EUR/GBP/USD pairs. Good for AUD, NZD, and JPY pairs. Know your session, know your pairs.

---

**Day 19** 💡
If your prop firm evaluation is at 8%+ profit in Phase 1, switch to ultra-conservative mode. 0.5% risk, 1-2 trades max. Don't give back what you've earned chasing 10%.

---

**Day 20** 💡
Drawdown tracker: https://eleusisfx.uk/resources/drawdown-tracker
Monitor your drawdown in real-time against your prop firm's limits. Don't rely on the prop firm's dashboard — it sometimes lags.

---

**Day 21** 💡
The market doesn't know you exist. It doesn't care about your entry, your profit target, or your rent. Trade what the chart shows, not what you need.

---

**Day 22** 💡
Mondays are often range days — the market is "deciding" direction. Tuesdays and Wednesdays typically offer the cleanest trending moves. Plan your week accordingly.

---

**Day 23** 💡
A 1% risk with a 3:1 R:R means you need to win just 26% of your trades to break even. Think about that. You can be wrong 3 out of 4 times and still pass your evaluation.

---

**Day 24** 💡
End-of-month rebalancing (last 2-3 trading days) creates unusual volatility. Institutional flows move against retail. Either reduce size or sit out.

---

**Day 25** 💡
Your trading plan should fit on one page. If it's more complex than that, you'll abandon it under pressure. Simple systems survive. Complex ones break.

---

**Day 26** 💡
Risk-reward calculator: https://eleusisfx.uk/resources/risk-reward-calculator
Check every trade BEFORE entry. If the R:R is below 2:1, skip it. No exceptions.

---

**Day 27** 💡
The best evaluation traders take 8-12 trades per WEEK, not per day. Quality > quantity. Every single time.

---

**Day 28** 💡
Don't check your P&L during a trade. Set your stop, set your target, walk away. Watching every tick creates emotional interference.

---

**Day 29** 💡
If you've failed an evaluation, the answer is almost never "better strategy." It's almost always: smaller size, fewer trades, and better risk management.

---

**Day 30** 💡
Getting funded is a process, not an event. If you pass — congratulations. If you don't, learn and try again. Or let professionals handle it: https://eleusisfx.uk
