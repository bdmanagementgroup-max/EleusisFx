# Article 3: FTMO Drawdown Rules Explained

📚 **FTMO Drawdown Rules: Trailing vs Static (2026)**
*Tag: Prop Firms*

This is the #1 rule traders misunderstand:

**Daily Drawdown (5%)**
• Resets every day at midnight CE(S)T
• Based on the HIGHER of: starting balance or highest equity that day
• ⚠️ Includes floating/unrealized losses

**Max Drawdown (10%)**
• Based on initial balance (static, not trailing)
• If you start at $100k, your floor is $90k — forever
• Even if you grow to $110k, floor stays at $90k

**The trap:** Many traders think daily drawdown resets from the starting balance. It doesn't — it resets from the highest equity reached that day. A profitable morning can shrink your afternoon risk allowance.

# Article 5: The 3-Trade Rule

📚 **The 3-Trade Rule: Why Overtrading Kills Evaluations**
*Tag: Strategy*

One of the most powerful rules in prop firm trading:

**The Rule:** Never take more than 3 trades in a single session.

**Why it works:**
1. **Prevents revenge trading** — After 2 losses, you walk away
2. **Forces selectivity** — You only take A+ setups
3. **Protects drawdown** — 3 losses at 1% risk = only 3% down
4. **Reduces emotional decisions** — Less screen time = less impulse

**The math:** If your win rate is 55% and your R:R is 2:1, you only need 1 winner out of 3 trades to be profitable for the day. Taking 10+ trades doesn't improve your odds — it increases variance and emotional risk.

Professional evaluation traders live by this rule. It's boring. It works.
