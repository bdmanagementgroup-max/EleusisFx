## Post 2: FTMO Drawdown Rules Explained

**Tag: ⚠️ Risk Management**

# FTMO Drawdown Rules — The #1 Reason Traders Fail

Two limits. Break either one and your challenge is over instantly.

**Daily Loss Limit (5%)**
Your equity cannot drop more than 5% from the START of any trading day. Not from your peak that day — from the opening balance at midnight server time.

Example: $100k account, day starts at $102,000.
→ Your floor for the day is $96,900 (5% of $102k)
→ If equity touches $96,900 at any point, even for a second — done.

**Maximum Drawdown (10%)**
Your equity can never fall more than 10% below your INITIAL starting balance.
→ $100k account? Your absolute floor is $90,000. Forever. It does not reset.

**The trap:** As you profit, your daily limit rises but your max drawdown stays fixed at $90k. Traders who run up to $108k think they have room — but one 8% drop day puts them right at the max drawdown.

## Post 3: Why Most Traders Fail Their Evaluation

**Tag: 🧠 Psychology**

# Why 90% of Traders Fail Their Prop Firm Evaluation

It is almost never about strategy. It is about behaviour under pressure.

**The 3 killers:**

1️⃣ **Revenge trading** — You take a loss. Instead of walking away, you double your lot size. Two more losses later, you have hit the daily drawdown limit.

2️⃣ **No position sizing system** — If you cannot calculate your lot size from your stop loss in under 10 seconds, you are guessing. Guessing kills accounts.

3️⃣ **Trading the clock** — You are 3 weeks in, only at 4% profit, and need 10%. So you start taking lower-quality setups to "catch up." The drawdown follows.

**The fix:** Treat the evaluation like a process, not a deadline. If your strategy averages 5% per month, a 10% target in 30 days is aggressive. You need either a higher win rate or someone who specialises in passing evaluations.

# The 3-Trade Rule That Keeps Funded Traders Funded

Simple rule. Massive impact.

## Post 9: Position Sizing 101

**Tag: 🧮 Tools**

# Free Position Size Calculator

Stop guessing your lot sizes. Use our free calculator.

**What you need:**
• Account balance (e.g. $100,000)
• Risk per trade (e.g. 1% = $1,000)
• Stop loss in pips (e.g. 30 pips)
• Pip value for your pair

**Quick formula:**
Lot size = Risk amount ÷ (Stop loss pips × Pip value)

Example: $100k account, 1% risk, 30 pip stop on EUR/USD
→ $1,000 ÷ (30 × $10) = 3.33 lots
