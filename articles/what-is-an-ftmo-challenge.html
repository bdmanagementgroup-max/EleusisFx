What Is an FTMO Challenge and How Does It Work?

This guide covers everything — from the basics of what a prop firm challenge is, to the specific FTMO rules that catch most traders out.

What Is a Prop Firm Challenge?

A proprietary trading firm (prop firm) provides capital to traders who can demonstrate consistent, rule-compliant profitability. Rather than hiring traders as employees, firms like FTMO use an evaluation model — you pay a fee to take a "challenge," prove you can trade profitably within their rules, and in return receive access to a funded trading account worth significantly more than your fee.

Think of it as a skills-based audition. The prop firm sets the parameters. You trade. If you hit the targets without breaking the rules, you get funded and keep a percentage of all future profits.

The core concept: You trade a demo account under real conditions and real rules. Pass, and you get access to a funded live account — typically $25,000 to $200,000 — where you keep 80–90% of every payout.

The FTMO Challenge: A Two-Phase Evaluation

The FTMO evaluation consists of two distinct phases. Both must be completed successfully before you receive a funded account. There's no skipping phases, no shortcuts. You must demonstrate consistency across both.

PHASE 01

The FTMO Challenge

  • Profit target: 10%
  • Max daily loss: 5%
  • Max overall loss: 10%
  • Minimum trading days: 4
  • Time limit: 30 calendar days
PHASE 02

The Verification

  • Profit target: 5%
  • Max daily loss: 5%
  • Max overall loss: 10%
  • Minimum trading days: 4
  • Time limit: 60 calendar days

Once both phases are passed, FTMO creates a real funded account in your name. From that point forward, you're trading with FTMO's capital and receiving up to 90% of every profit payout.

Understanding the Rules in Detail

The rules are where most traders come unstuck. FTMO tracks every position, every day, in real time. There are no grace periods, no appeals — if you breach a rule, the challenge ends immediately.

Rule Limit What It Means
Daily Loss Limit 5% Your account balance cannot drop more than 5% in a single trading day. This resets daily at midnight CET.
Maximum Loss (Drawdown) 10% Your account can never fall more than 10% below its initial starting balance. This is a hard stop.
Profit Target P1 10% You must grow the account by 10% during Phase 1 to qualify for Phase 2.
Profit Target P2 5% A lower target for Phase 2, giving you room to trade more conservatively.
Minimum Trading Days 4 days You cannot hit the profit target in 1–3 days. You must trade on at least 4 separate days.
Time Limit 30 / 60 days 30 days for Phase 1, 60 days for Phase 2. If you don't hit the target in time, the challenge ends.

Common Rules People Misunderstand

The Daily Loss Limit Is Based on Balance, Not Equity

Many traders assume the 5% daily loss limit is calculated from their current equity at the start of each day. It's actually calculated from your account balance — the figure excluding open positions. This means an open trade that swings against you can push you over the limit faster than you expect if you're not accounting for it correctly.

News Trading Restrictions

FTMO restricts trading during major news events. Specifically, you cannot hold positions open during high-impact news releases like NFP, FOMC decisions, or CPI data. Many traders get caught by this rule when they leave trades running through news they didn't notice on the calendar.

Weekend Holding

FTMO prohibits holding positions over the weekend. All trades must be closed before the market closes on Friday. This is a hard rule — any open positions at close will result in an automatic fail.

EA and Algorithmic Trading

Expert Advisors (EAs) are allowed, but only on one account at a time. If FTMO detects the same EA running across multiple accounts in a way that looks like coordinated arbitrage or risk exploitation, the account will be flagged and likely terminated.

Why Do So Many Traders Fail?

The FTMO pass rate is notoriously low — studies suggest fewer than 10% of traders who start a challenge ever receive a funded account. The reasons are consistent: overleveraging early in the challenge, emotional trading after a losing streak, violating the daily loss limit on a single bad day, and misjudging the consistency required across both phases.

The irony is that the rules themselves aren't particularly restrictive for a disciplined trader. The challenge is psychological. When real money (even just the evaluation fee) is on the line, traders deviate from their strategy. That's what ends most challenges.

The key insight: FTMO doesn't fail traders because the rules are impossible. It fails traders because most people abandon their strategy under pressure. The evaluation is as much a psychological test as a technical one.

Is the FTMO Challenge Worth It?

For a trader with a proven strategy and the discipline to execute it, yes — the economics are genuinely attractive. A $100,000 funded account with an 80% profit split means you keep $8,000 from every 10% profit month. That's a significant return on a ~€540 evaluation fee.

The problem is most traders don't have a proven strategy. They have a strategy that works sometimes, in certain conditions, when they're not stressed. That's a very different thing. And that gap is exactly where most challenges fail.
