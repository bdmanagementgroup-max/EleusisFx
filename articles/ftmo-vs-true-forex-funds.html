FTMO vs True Forex Funds: Which Prop Firm Is Right for You?

Eleusis FX — April 2025 — 9 min read
Back to Articles

FTMO and True Forex Funds are two of the most established names in the prop trading space. Both offer two-phase evaluation models, both fund traders at up to $100,000+, and both have genuine track records of paying out. But they're not the same. The differences in their rules, fee structures, payout models and trader restrictions matter — and choosing the wrong firm for your trading style can be the difference between passing and failing.

At Eleusis FX, we've successfully completed evaluations with both firms. This is our ground-level comparison — not marketing copy from either firm's website, but an honest breakdown of what actually matters when you're deciding where to take your challenge.

Quick answer: FTMO is the gold standard for credibility and payout consistency. True Forex Funds offers more flexible rules and a slightly more forgiving evaluation — better for traders with wider stop losses or less rigid strategies. For most UK clients, we recommend FTMO.

Where FTMO Wins

FTMO has been operating since 2014. That decade of track record matters — they've paid out hundreds of millions of dollars to traders and their reputation for actually paying is essentially unquestioned. When you're handing over a funded account to trade, that trust matters enormously.

The profit split is also better — up to 90% versus TFF's 80%. On a $100K account making 10% per month, that's $9,000 versus $8,000. Not enormous, but it compounds.

FTMO's scaling plan is also significantly more aggressive. You can scale to $2 million in funded capital through their programme, versus $400K with TFF. For traders with long-term ambitions, that ceiling matters.

FTMO is the better choice if:

FTMO

Best For Structured, Rule-Disciplined Traders

  • You have a clear intraday strategy that closes trades daily
  • You don't trade during major news events
  • You want the highest profit split available (up to 90%)
  • Long-term scaling is a priority — FTMO allows up to $2M
  • Credibility and payout track record matter to you
  • You're a UK-based trader who wants the most recognised prop firm name

Where True Forex Funds Wins

TFF's biggest advantage is flexibility. They allow news trading — meaning you can hold positions through NFP, FOMC and other high-impact releases without any risk of a rule violation. For news traders or macro traders, this alone makes TFF significantly more compatible.

Weekend holding is also permitted. Swing traders who take multi-day or multi-week positions will find TFF far more accommodating. With FTMO, any position held over the weekend is an automatic violation.

The Phase 1 profit target is also lower — 8% versus FTMO's 10%. That might seem minor, but in practice it's a meaningful reduction in required performance. Combined with the 45-day Phase 1 window (versus FTMO's 30 days), TFF's evaluation is genuinely more achievable for traders with less aggressive strategies.

Payouts also come faster — after 14 days on the funded account versus FTMO's 30 days. If cash flow is a consideration, TFF has a meaningful advantage here.

True Forex Funds is the better choice if:

True Forex Funds

Best For Flexible, Event-Driven Traders

  • You trade during or around major news events
  • You use a swing trading strategy with multi-day holds
  • You want faster access to your first payout (14 days vs 30)
  • A lower Phase 1 profit target suits your strategy (8% vs 10%)
  • You use EAs or algorithms and want fewer restrictions
  • You're comfortable with a slightly lower profit split in exchange for more flexibility

The Credibility Question

This matters more than most comparison articles acknowledge. FTMO has been paying traders since 2014. True Forex Funds was founded in 2021. That doesn't make TFF untrustworthy — they have a solid reputation and genuine payout history. But in an industry that has seen firms disappear overnight (My Forex Funds was shut down by regulators in 2023 without warning), the age and track record of a firm is a meaningful factor.

Our position: Both firms are legitimate and both pay. But if we had to recommend one for a first-time prop firm client, it would be FTMO — not because TFF is inferior, but because FTMO's decade of track record removes a category of risk that simply doesn't need to exist.

Fees: What You Actually Pay

Both firms refund the evaluation fee on your first profit split after passing. So in theory, the fee is temporary — it's returned to you. In practice, fees are only refunded if you pass, and most traders don't pass on the first attempt.

FTMO's $100K challenge fee is approximately €540. TFF's is approximately $499. They're comparable. The fee isn't the deciding factor — the rules, the payout model, and the compatibility with your strategy are.

Our Verdict

If you're a structured intraday trader with clear entry and exit rules, FTMO is the better firm. Better profit split, better scaling ceiling, better credibility, and rules that are entirely manageable for disciplined execution.

If you're a swing trader, a news trader, or someone whose strategy doesn't fit neatly into FTMO's news and weekend restrictions, True Forex Funds is a genuinely solid alternative with a lower evaluation bar and faster payouts.
